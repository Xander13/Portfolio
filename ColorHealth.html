01
The designs

Revamped for the Color cancer pages with new illustrations, iconography treatment, fresh design systems, and improved layouts




Illustrations with people smiling helps put users at ease avoiding the fear in reading cancer facts.




Adding secondary color tones to iconography to help users comprehend the context.




Improved designs for presenting data in a clear and visually engaging manner.






02
Overview



Problem


How might we improve the click rates to encourage users to buy more DNA kits to help understand and take preventive measures for potential future cancers?

Outcome


We refreshed old existing cancer pages with an updated design system, new illustrations, and shared new ideas on how they can expand their design system to other areas of the site.

Research


Our team has researched how color fits into the health market matrix and explored design assets to improve their brand strategies.




Old Color website










03
Features





Custom illustrations help build the Color brand stronger.

Color aims to provide health information to users to ensure they feel at ease when reading about difficult topics.


The old site had no supporting visuals for users to connect with the readings. It was all heavy data and text-based.





People with smiles help users see that they can be happy while learning from the Color pages. This helps avoid churn and reduces fear when reading about cancer facts.










I have explored ways to enhance Color's new design language system, as it was lacking visual significance for the users.




Red emphasizes harm, as the article explains the detrimental effects of diseases.



Icons with no color background doesn't provide enough emphasis for users to grasp the context at a quick glance.
