Halo lighting

HALO Lighting is an industrial design concept project focused on creating more accessible light sources that travel with you.


Challenges

Light sources tend to be very static. Halo aims to free range light sources where ever users go.

Constraints

Learn to design a physical product with UX skills

Role

Concept, Industrial Design,
Research, prototyping

Timeline

6 Months V1 design,
In progress V2 design


01
The designs




Halo lighting app puts user in full control how they want to luminate their lighting system.






02
The Concept



Problem


How might we improve the lighting sources that follows users for atypical environments?

Outcome


I've designed a drone approach light system that will afloat over peoples head to illuminate light the space.

Research


I have researched a lot in understanding how drones system works so I can break the rules to create a new product.









Halo light strongest advantage is that it doesn't depend on walls or tables to luminate lights to the environment.







Halo lighting is primarily focused on designing for the inclusion community.

03
The design
Explorations





Sci-Fi movies and tv shows has always inspires me to create things that are near impossible to something close to reality.







V1 design had a much thicker light ring to help luminate much larger.


The issue with V1 design, was the weight load which made it harder for drone to pick up all the materials and electronics.

V2 design had a much slimmer design to reduce the weight factor.


I've created the Halo ring to rotate in different axis to showcase it's versatility on how the light should aid to the environment.

Designing for physical products has a different approach to thinking which I enjoy learning new factors to learn.

I have learned to design with feelings and then design baed with measurements. utilizing both is a great tools to be free but being precise to make things work.

I have learned a lot in non-design learning such as drone systems, understanding basic electronics power usages, and thinking like an industrial designer.





As now Halo lighting can fly at fixed setting, the next steps is creating a more robust system that follows users where ever it goes.
